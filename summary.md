# The Daily Ops Report (DOR)

*A quick guide for directors — what it is, why it matters, and what's in it for you.*

---

## What it is

The Daily Ops Report is a simple web app where each director records the key
numbers and notes from their day — attendance, labor, enrollment and marketing
activity, staffing, the director packet, and a short report on the day.

It replaces the weekly copy-a-spreadsheet routine. One report per site, per day,
filed in about **two minutes**. It saves as you type, and the moment you hit
submit, the information is instantly available to leadership.

**Live at:** [bbdor.netlify.app](https://bbdor.netlify.app) ·
**How-to guide:** [bbdor.netlify.app/how-to.html](https://bbdor.netlify.app/how-to.html)

---

## Why it matters

For years our daily numbers lived in spreadsheets we never went back to. The
data was there, but it was too scattered to learn from — so the insights, the
early warnings, and the wins all got lost.

The DOR fixes that. The same two minutes you already spend thinking about your
day now becomes something the whole organization can see and act on:

- **Nothing slips through the cracks.** A staffing issue, an overtime spike, a
  missed packet, a string of tours that aren't converting — these get noticed
  *today*, not three weeks later in a spreadsheet nobody opened.
- **The wins get seen too.** Enrollment growth, hitting your comms goal, a strong
  week — leadership sees the good news in real time, not just the problems.
- **It tells the story of your site.** Over time, the DOR becomes the record of
  how your site is really doing — trends, patterns, and progress you can point to.
- **It's one source of truth.** You, KP, and leadership are all looking at the
  same live picture instead of chasing different versions of a spreadsheet.

The honest pitch: **what you capture each day is only as valuable as it is
consistent.** A daily habit turns scattered numbers into real insight.

---

## What's in it for you

This isn't just paperwork — it's built to make the daily habit genuinely
rewarding, and to celebrate the directors who show up and do it well.

- **Build a streak.** File on each workday (Mon–Fri) and your streak grows. Miss
  a day? You get one free "streak freeze" each month, so a single sick day won't
  reset you.
- **Earn points and badges** for consistency and thoroughness — filing on time,
  filing before 6 PM (early-bird bonus), and writing complete notes.
- **Friendly competition.** All three sites share a weekly leaderboard, ranked on
  *consistency* — showing up and filing well — not on enrollment numbers you
  can't fully control. Plus a shared team goal everyone wins together.
- **Your effort is what counts.** Points reward the things you control: the habit
  and the quality of your report. A slow enrollment week never hurts your score.

---

## What's expected

- **One report per day, per site** — ideally before you head out, and before
  6 PM for the bonus.
- **Add the notes.** A number on its own only tells half the story. Whenever a
  count is above zero, add the names, rooms, or reasons. That's where the value is.
- **Be honest and complete.** The DOR works because the data is real. Even a
  quick, honest report keeps your streak alive and the picture accurate.

---

## How leadership uses it

KP and Rob get a live dashboard across all three sites: attendance and overtime
trends, the enrollment pipeline, staffing watch, packet compliance, red flags
that need attention, and celebrations worth sharing — all updating the instant
you submit. It's the cross-site view a copied spreadsheet could never give us.

---

## The bottom line

Two minutes a day, done consistently, gives every director a clear record of
their site, gives leadership real-time insight, and surfaces both the problems
and the wins while they still matter. Keep the chain going — and let's see who
takes the top of the leaderboard. 🔥
